# VZGLYD: 10 Items of the Covenant

*Collectively written, 2025. First published in full with the initial open
source release.*

---

**I.**

VZGLYD operates as a collective of constraint. The slide is not the author's
expression — the constraint is the author's expression. Each slide is a unit of
work produced according to the principles of limitation and discipline, where
the individual polygon does not speak: the vertex budget speaks. The work is
mathematical and the language is visual.

---

**II.**

VZGLYD analyzes the relation between hardware limits and visual culture in their
exhausted phase, presented through slides running on commodity hardware. The
work sublimates the tension between what is technically possible and what is
deliberately chosen — addressing the frustration of those who have worked
within unlimited budgets and found them insufficient — while eliminating direct
technological exhibitionism.

---

**III.**

All 3D art undergoes the manipulation of photorealism unless it speaks the
language of that manipulation itself, thereby revealing photorealism's
omnipresence in contemporary visual culture. A flat-shaded triangle on a
domestic television is more honest than ten million polygons pretending to
forget they are made of numbers.

---

**IV.**

Individual authorship is not erased by a mandatory visual effect; it is
redirected by equal limits. The vertex budget applies equally to every slide.
The texture budget applies equally to every slide. The shader contract applies
equally to every slide. The author works within these conditions; the author
does not set them.

---

**V.**

The vertex budget — 60,000 per slide — functions as the hierarchical directive
structure from which there is no appeal. The slide operates within this
structure or it does not operate. The Raspberry Pi 4 does not negotiate.

---

**VI.**

VZGLYD's material is not a fixed visual recipe. It is a fixed condition: bounded
geometry, bounded textures, a portable shader contract, and a renderer that
refuses to expand into everything at once. Composition is not the selection of
infinite resources; it is the decision of what matters inside a small space.

---

**VII.**

VZGLYD rejects the evolutionary trajectory of rendering — the arms race of
polygons, the pursuit of physically-based materials, the aspiration toward
photographic reproduction — and maintains a fixed set of constraints expressed
through contemporary hardware to produce not nostalgia but refusal: a formal
position held deliberately against the direction of the field.

---

**VIII.**

VZGLYD practices aesthetic provocation through deliberate restraint — the
performance of limitation in a context where limitation is not required. A
slide of four hundred triangles on a fifty-five inch television is not a
technical failure. It is a claim about what kind of attention is worth
demanding, and from whom.

---

**IX.**

Two sister systems constitute the formal law within which all slides operate.
*vzglyd-slide* is the law of form: the ABI contract by which slides speak to the
engine, stable and versioned, the boundary between what is permitted and what
is not. *vzglyd-sidecar* is the law of data: the channel by which the world
outside reaches the world inside, constrained to the socket, the request, the
response. Neither system speaks for itself. Both systems speak through the
slide.

---

**X.**

VZGLYD reveals the distance between what the world contains and what a vertex
budget can describe. The terrain slide encodes rolling hills, five biome zones,
chimney smoke, and the present price of bitcoin in 4,225 vertices — leaving
55,775 unused. The slide is not a record of reality. It is an argument about
which parts of reality are worth encoding. The constraint is what makes the
argument necessary.

---

*VZGLYD. Small worlds, well made.*
